🌊 Letters from a Seaside Desk - April 2026

April Reads, Quiet Inspiration & A Return to Mystery

My dear reader, April arrived softly, wrapped in rain, books, and inspiration. This month, I treated myself to a visit to the hairdresser and, naturally, to two new books joining my ever-growing library: Yesteryear and Atmosphere. Atmosphere had been on my wishlist for quite some time, while Yesteryear is already being whispered about as one of the standout books of the season — and naturally, I could not resist. I also immersed myself in the haunting world of Les Mystère d'Eversand La Suivante by Victor Dixen, the opening installment in a captivating four-book series. The story follows Birdie Baker, a young woman who leaves behind her life in Colombia to become a French maid within the enigmatic Rosemore family estate. Yet Eversand is far from what she imagined: behind every corridor lingers mystery, tension, and secrets waiting to surface. The atmosphere of the novel utterly consumed me, and I quickly continued through the series during May. There is something deeply addictive about stories layered with intrigue, hidden histories, and unsettling elegance. As spring unfolded, I also continued developing my own projects and exploring the stories that continue to call me back to mythology, history, and emotionally charged worlds. Yours, from a desk not far from the sea! Manon
